# Why Japan Leads the World in Longevity

A research report and supporting data on Japan's world-leading life expectancy
and the diet, healthcare, lifestyle, and social factors behind it.

_Compiled June 2026 using 2025 figures._

## Key findings
- Japan's overall life expectancy is **~84.8 years** — women **87.1** (#1 worldwide for 40 straight years), men **81.1**.
- Japan outlives the United States by **~5 years** despite far lower healthcare spending per capita — the "spending paradox."
- Longevity is driven by a reinforcing system: traditional diet, universal preventive healthcare, active daily life, portion culture (*hara hachi bu*), and strong community/purpose (*ikigai*, *moai*).
- Caveats: diets are westernizing, traditional food is high in salt, and the aging population strains pensions and care. Migrant studies show lifestyle outweighs genetics.
